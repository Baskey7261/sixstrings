Welcome.

At Musical Six Strings, we specialize in curriculum-based guitar tuition, which encompasses the Trinity College of London's Classical & Plectrum grade initial through to grade 8 syllabus (2020-2023).

Having a Trinity grade certificate verifies that you have met the criteria required by the Regulated Qualifications Framework (RQF). Credentials such as these are recognized around the globe as the industry standard in the music and entertainments industry.

Grading System:

As set by the Regulated Qualifications Framework.

The grading system begins at grade initial for beginners and continues through to grade 8. Every pupil at Musical Six Strings will undergo an initial assessment to determine their current grade level in accordance with, the Trinity College of London (TCL) syllabus.

Grades 6-8 are a minimum requirement for students hoping to be put forward for further education such as a diploma or higher. We encourage our pupils to work toward completing each grade, however, we do also cater to pupils that are just aiming to hone their current skills and expand on their musical repertoire.

Our syllabuses:

We run two main syllabuses, Classical & Rock School; although, we do offer classes in flamenco, ukulele, or none instrument-specific lessons in music theory and sight-reading.

Our curriculum aims to not only expand on the student's guitar playing technique but to also provide in-depth knowledge of notation and the instrument they are playing, along with the ability to improvise fluently and creatively in response to unexpected musical stimuli.

Rock School:

Designed around the Trinity College of London's Rock and Pop syllabus (2018-2024).

Rock School:

Designed around the Trinity College of London's Rock and Pop syllabus (2018-2024).

The Rock School syllabus focuses on merging the techniques required for both acoustic and electric guitar. The aim of the syllabus is to build a skill set that will be useful in building a repertoire that suits both instruments,

with an emphasis on a variety of finger styles and plectrum technique. Students will be encouraged to explore a range of styles including, rock and pop, jazz, blues, and country. The syllabus also covers chord progressions and patterns, along with standard and none-standard, scales and arpeggios, incorporating the use of both plectrum and fingers. with emphasis on both finger styles and plectrum technique.
